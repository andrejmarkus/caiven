# Caiven creator rights

Caiven is intended to be a creator-friendly fantasy console. This document states the project's policy for games and cartridges made with Caiven.

## Your games are yours

You retain ownership of the original game code, artwork, music, sound effects, writing, and other content you create for a Caiven cartridge.

Using Caiven does not transfer ownership of your work to the Caiven project or its maintainers.

## Commercial use is allowed

You may publish, sell, license, or otherwise commercially exploit games made with Caiven without:

- paying royalties or revenue share to the Caiven project;
- purchasing a separate commercial-use licence;
- asking the Caiven maintainers for permission; or
- publishing your game's source code.

You are responsible for ensuring that third-party code, assets, fonts, music, and other material included in your game permit your intended use.

## Cartridge files and exported games

A `.cav` cartridge created by a user is user content; the project does not claim it is a derivative work of Caiven solely because it targets the Caiven runtime or uses Caiven's public APIs.

When Caiven runtime code is copied into or distributed with an exported game, that runtime code remains subject to its own licence. The game's separate code and assets may use terms chosen by the creator, subject to the licences of any third-party material they include.

## Optional services

Official hosting, cloud features, support, asset packs, signed builds, or other convenience services may be offered for a fee. Payment for optional services does not change ownership of a creator's games.

## No platform lock-in promise

The project intends to keep the local editor, runtime, cartridge loading and saving, and normal game distribution usable without a mandatory online account or revenue share.
